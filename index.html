About Me

I am currently a Life Sciences Research Professional in the lab of Dr. Glaivy Batsuli at Stanford School of Medicine, where I study adaptive immune dynamics using murine models and single-cell RNA sequencing.

My background spans computational modeling, in vitro, and in vivo approaches applied to signaling and learning-like processes across the adaptive immune system, gut microbiome, and neural systems. I aim to develop quantitative models — drawing on frameworks such as predictive coding, reinforcement learning, and network dynamics — to explain how biological systems encode, update, and act on information across scales.
