About Me

My name is Eamon O’Connor, and I am excited to use bioinformatics to help develop novel vaccines and therapeutics designed to improve the outlook and quality of life for patients.

I decided to pursue a master’s in bioinformatics shortly after switching from engineering to biology while pursuing my bachelor’s. Combined, these experiences taught me both the mathematics and biological principles necessary to be a skilled bioinformatician.

My research experience includes analysis of Next Generation Sequencing data and studying how biological systems are affected by disease, using scripts written in Python and R as well as tools such as seqkit and CellChat. Looking ahead I am excited to explore opportunities in vaccine and drug discovery, where I can apply bioinformatics to identify drug targets and optimize therapeutic strategies.

Outside of work I enjoy reading, travelling, and cooking. I am currently based in the Greater Boston area but am actively exploring opportunities in California and overseas. I am open to both on-site and remote opportunities.

Feel free to reach out with any questions or inquiries! You can contact me at oconnor.eamon0@gmail.com

Work

My personal and academic research focuses on understanding how disease impacts biological systems, particularly the immune system and gut microbiome. I achieved this using scripts written in R and Python, which are available to view on my Github (linked above).
