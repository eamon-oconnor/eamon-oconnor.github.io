About Me

My name is Eamon O’Connor and I hope to improve people’s lives through a career in bioinformatics.

I have a background in Philosophy, Civil Engineering, and Veterinary Medicine. While these experiences were instrumental in developing my ability to communicate, problem solve, and apply biological principles in the real world, I felt that I could be doing more.

I discovered my passion for bioinformatics during the last year of my undergraduate studies, which led me to pursue a Master’s degree in the field. This path presents an alignment between my interests and talents that will allow me to make a positive impact that is both far-reaching and significant.

My experience includes analyzing Next Generation Sequencing data and studying how biological systems are affected by disease, using scripts written in Python and R as well as tools such as Seqkit and CellChat. Looking ahead I am excited to explore opportunities in vaccine and drug discovery where I can apply bioinformatics to identify drug targets and optimize therapeutic strategies.

Outside of work I enjoy reading, travelling, and cooking. I am currently based in the Greater Boston area but am actively exploring opportunities in California and overseas. I am open to both on-site and remote opportunities.

Feel free to reach out with any questions or inquiries! You can contact me at oconnor.eamon0@gmail.com

Work

My personal and academic research focuses on understanding how disease impacts biological systems, particularly the immune system and gut microbiome. I achieved this using scripts written in R and Python, which are available to view on my Github (linked above).
